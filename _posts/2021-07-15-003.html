What happened in the last month

People Staring at Computers kylemcdonald.net

Google [Ads] bans privacy-first products dkzlv.com
Google and Microsoft have super opaque and unpredictable ad moderation. There are unofficial policies that indirectly ban the advertising of privacy-first services.
Kill the standard privacy notice techcrunch.com
Google and Microsoft have super opaque and unpredictable ad moderation. There are unofficial policies that indirectly ban the advertising of privacy-first services.
ADPC: A new HTTP spec proposes elimination of obnoxious "cookie banners" arstechnica.com
A new proposed HTTP standard by noyb.eu and CSL would allow the user to set their privacy preferences once, inside the browser itself, and have the browser communicate those preferences invisibly with any website the user visits.
Reclaim your face techcrunch.com
Sign the petition for this civil society initiative for a ban on biometric mass surveillance practices. Only European Union citizens allowed.
New privacy apps

Mylio
Mylio sync your photos on all your devices. Thumbnails on your phone, originals on your computer. It also keeps your current folder structure, so there is no vendor lock-in.
Brave Search
Most of us know the browser Brave already. Recently they also created their own search engine.
hCaptcha
Do you use a captcha to keep out bots? hCaptcha protects user privacy, rewards websites, and helps companies get their data labeled. It is a drop-in replacement for reCAPTCHA.
